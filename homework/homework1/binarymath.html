Generally when a person does basic arithmetic, they do math in a base of ten.
Binary numbers, however, are in a base of two. That means that numbers
are represented by only two digits. Those two digits are 1's and 0's.
As a comparison, numbers in a base of ten are represented by ten digits
(e.g. 0,1,2,3,4,5,6,7,8)


So how does one count in a base of two? Well lets look at the following table
to see how numbers are represented in binary:

NUMBER BINARY
0 0
1 1
2 10
3 11
4 100
5 101


As you can deduce, the first digit represents 1. The second digit represents 2.
The third, 4. The fourth, 8. And so on. To better visualize this, look at
the above table for the number five, which is represented in binary as 101.
We would look at the three digits and see 4 + 0 + 1 = 5. Let's take the binary
number 7, this would be represented by forming digits that represent 4 + 2 + 1.
Thus the answer is 111 in binary. And the number 6 would be 110, or 4+2+0 = 6.
